Steve Jobs

Visionary Innovator: Steve Jobs - Revolutionizing Technology, Inspiring Generations

About the Legend

1955: Steve Jobs is born on February 24 in San Francisco, California.
1972: Jobs enrolls at Reed College in Oregon but drops out after six months.
1974: Jobs returns to California and begins attending meetings of the Homebrew Computer Club.
1976: Jobs co-founds Apple Computer, Inc. with Steve Wozniak in the Jobs family garage.
1977: Apple releases the Apple II, a groundbreaking personal computer that becomes a commercial success.
1980: Apple goes public with its initial public offering (IPO), making Jobs a multimillionaire at the age of 25.
1983: Apple releases the Lisa, the first commercially available computer with a graphical user interface (GUI).
1984: Apple introduces the Macintosh, a user-friendly computer that popularizes the mouse and GUI.
1985: Jobs resigns from Apple after a power struggle with the board of directors and starts NeXT Inc., a computer company.
1986: Jobs acquires Pixar Animation Studios from George Lucas and becomes its majority shareholder.
1995: Pixar releases the world's first full-length computer-animated feature film, "Toy Story."
1996: Apple acquires NeXT Inc., and Jobs returns to Apple as an advisor.
1997: Jobs becomes the interim CEO of Apple, working to revitalize the company.
1998: Apple releases the iMac, a consumer-friendly computer that rejuvenates the company's image.
2001: Apple introduces the iPod, a portable media player that revolutionizes the music industry .
2003: Apple launches the iTunes Store, transforming the way music is purchased and consumed.
2007: Apple releases the iPhone, a revolutionary smartphone that combines a mobile phone, iPod, and internet device.
2010: Apple introduces the iPad, a tablet computer that creates a new market for portable devices.
2011: Steve Jobs passes away on October 5 at the age of 56 due to complications from pancreatic cancer.
Steve Jobs' contributions to technology, innovation, and design continue to inspire and shape the world we live in today.
